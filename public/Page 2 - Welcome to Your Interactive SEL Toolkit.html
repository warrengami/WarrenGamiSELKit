Welcome to Your Interactive SEL Toolkit

Engaging, Research-Based Tools

This toolkit provides everything you need to bring social-emotional learning to life through interactive play and meaningful conversations. Designed for grades 3-8, these resources help students develop key SEL competencies in a fun, engaging way.

What's Inside:

  • Printable discussion dice with age-appropriate prompts
  • Scenario cards for real-world practice
  • Teacher implementation guide with tips
  • Calm, origami-inspired design

Quick Start Guide

First Week: Introduce 1-2 dice categories and 1 scenario format. Focus on establishing norms.

Daily Use: 5-10 minutes for morning check-ins or transitions using dice prompts.

Weekly Use: 20-30 minutes for deeper scenario discussions or role-plays.

Teacher Tip ▼

Start small! Choose one component to implement for the first 2 weeks before adding more. This helps students build comfort with the format.

CASEL Competencies Addressed

Click on each competency below to reveal more details and example prompts.

1

Self-Awareness

Identifying emotions, accurate self-perception

Focus: Understanding one's own emotions, thoughts, and values.

  • Example prompt: "When do you feel most proud of yourself?"
  • Example prompt: "Name an emotion that can be tricky to handle."
2

Self-Management

Impulse control, stress management

Focus: Regulating one's emotions, thoughts, and behaviors effectively.

  • Example prompt: "What is one thing you can do to calm down when you're upset?"
  • Example prompt: "Share a time you had to be patient to reach a goal."
3

Social Awareness

Perspective-taking, empathy

Focus: Understanding the perspectives of and empathizing with others.

  • Example prompt: "How can you tell how a friend is feeling without them saying anything?"
  • Example prompt: "Describe a situation from someone else's point of view."
4

Relationship Skills

Communication, conflict resolution

Focus: Establishing and maintaining healthy and supportive relationships.

  • Example prompt: "What are the ingredients for a good friendship?"
  • Example prompt: "How can you disagree with someone respectfully?"
5

Responsible Decision-Making

Problem-solving, ethical responsibility

Focus: Making caring and constructive choices about personal behavior.

  • Example prompt: "What's one rule at school that helps everyone stay safe?"
  • Example prompt: "Describe a time you had to make a tough but fair choice."
