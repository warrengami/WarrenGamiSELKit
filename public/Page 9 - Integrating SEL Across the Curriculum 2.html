Integrating SEL Across the Curriculum

Weave social-emotional learning into your daily academic instruction to reinforce skills in context.

Filter by Subject:

Character Analysis

Use dice prompts to discuss characters' feelings, motivations, and decisions.

Author's Purpose & Empathy

Analyze why an author made a character act a certain way to make the reader feel an emotion.

Perspective Writing

Have students rewrite a scene from a different character's point of view.

Structured Debate

Use discussion norms (p. 3) for structured debates on topics from a novel or article.

Historical Empathy

Discuss the perspectives and feelings of different groups during historical events.

Civic Dialogue

Use the discussion norms for respectful debates on historical or current events.

Analyzing Primary Sources

When reading historical letters or diaries, identify the emotions and perspectives of the writer.

Group Work Norms

Apply scenario card strategies to lab groups or collaborative math problems.

Growth Mindset

Discuss frustration and perseverance when facing challenging multi-step problems.

Data Storytelling

Use charts and data to tell a human story. "What do these numbers tell us about people's lives?"

Emotion Wheels

In Visual Arts, create color wheels or drawings to represent different feelings.

Role-Playing Prompts

In Drama, use scenario cards as prompts for role-playing and improvisational skits.

Music & Mood

Play different musical pieces and have students describe or draw the emotions they evoke.
