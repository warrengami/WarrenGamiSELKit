üó£Ô∏è Ready-to-Use Scripts

Opening Scripts

For Dice Activities:
"Today we're going to use these special dice to help us talk about our feelings and how we work together. When I roll the dice, we'll discuss the question that comes up. There are no wrong answers - we're here to learn from each other!"
For Scenario Cards:
"I'm going to show you a situation that might happen in real life. We'll talk about what we would do and why. This helps us practice making good decisions together."

Transition Scripts

Between Activities:
"Great discussion! Let's take a moment to think about what we learned before we move on to our next activity."
For Group Work:
"Now let's work with a partner to share our thoughts. Remember to listen carefully to what your partner says."

Closure Scripts

End of Activity:
"Thank you for sharing your thoughts today. Remember, these skills help us not just in class, but everywhere we go!"

üìè Activity Timing Guide

Quick Activities (5-10 minutes)

  • Morning Meeting: One dice roll or scenario card
  • Transition Time: Quick reflection question
  • Exit Ticket: "What did you learn about yourself today?"

Full Activities (15-20 minutes)

  • Multiple Dice Rolls: 2-3 different prompts
  • Scenario Discussion: Deep dive with follow-up questions
  • Group Work: Partner or small group discussions

üí° Pro Tips for Success

Start Small: Begin with 5-minute activities and gradually increase time as students become comfortable.
Be Consistent: Use SEL activities regularly (even just once a week) to build a positive classroom culture.
Model Vulnerability: Share your own thoughts and feelings when appropriate to show it's okay to be open.
Celebrate Growth: Notice and acknowledge when students use SEL skills in real situations.
